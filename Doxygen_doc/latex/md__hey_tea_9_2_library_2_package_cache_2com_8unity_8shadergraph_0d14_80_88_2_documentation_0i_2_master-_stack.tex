The Master Stack is the end point of a Shader Graph that defines the final surface appearance of a shader. Your Shader Graph should always contain only one Master Stack.



The content of the Master Stack might change depending on the \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_graph-_settings-_tab}{Graph Settings}{0} you select. The Master Stack is made up of Contexts, which contain \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_block-_node}{Block nodes}{0}.\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_master-_stack_autotoc_md3438}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Contexts}{Contexts}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_master-_stack_autotoc_md3438}


The Master Stack contains two Contexts\+: Vertex and Fragment. These represent the two stages of a shader. Nodes that you connect to Blocks in the Vertex Context become part of the final shader\textquotesingle{}s vertex function. Nodes that you connect to Blocks in the Fragment Context become part of the final shader\textquotesingle{}s fragment (or pixel) function. If you connect any nodes to both Contexts, they are executed twice, once in the vertex function and then again in the fragment function. You can\textquotesingle{}t cut, copy, or paste Contexts.
