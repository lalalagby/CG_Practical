The Motion Blur effect simulates the blur that occurs in an image when a real-\/world camera films objects moving faster than the cameraâ€™s exposure time. This is usually due to rapidly moving objects, or a long exposure time.

Universal Render Pipeline (URP) only blurs camera motions.\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_doaa46dc21e25ec0285513513fcd03eb27_autotoc_md2305}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Using Motion Blur}{Using Motion Blur}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_doaa46dc21e25ec0285513513fcd03eb27_autotoc_md2305}
{\bfseries{Motion Blur}} uses the \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} system, so to enable and modify {\bfseries{Motion Blur}} properties, you must add a {\bfseries{Motion Blur}} override to a \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} in your Scene. To add {\bfseries{Motion Blur}} to a Volume\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item In the Scene or Hierarchy view, select a Game\+Object that contains a Volume component to view it in the Inspector.
\item In the Inspector, navigate to {\bfseries{Add Override \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} Post-\/processing}}, and click on {\bfseries{Motion Blur}}. URP now applies {\bfseries{Motion Blur}} to any Camera this Volume affects.

To decrease the performance impact of Motion Blur, you can\+:


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Reduce the {\bfseries{Quality}}. A lower quality setting gives higher performance but may exhibit more visual artifacts.
\item Decrease the {\bfseries{Clamp}} to reduce the maximum velocity that \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} takes into account. Lower values give higher performance. 
\end{DoxyItemize}
