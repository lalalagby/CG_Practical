 The Depth Of Field component applies a depth of field effect, which simulates the focus properties of a camera lens. In real life, a camera can only focus sharply on an object at a specific distance. Objects nearer or farther from the camera are out of focus. The blurring gives a visual cue about an object’s distance, and introduces “bokeh”, which refers to visual artifacts that appear around bright areas of the image as they fall out of focus. To read more about bokeh, see the \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bokeh}{\texttt{ Wikipedia article on Bokeh}}.

The Universal Render Pipeline (URP) has two depth of field modes\+:


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{Gaussian}}\+: this mode approximates camera-\/like effects, but doesn’t imitate them completely. It has a limited blur radius and only does far-\/field blurring. This mode is the fastest, and is the best mode for lower-\/end platforms.~\newline

\item {\bfseries{Bokeh}}\+: a slower but higher quality mode that closely imitates the effects of a real-\/life camera. It can do both near \& far-\/field blurring, and generates bokeh on areas with high luminosity intensity, also known as hot spots.~\newline

\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_docf2fa11f09b546737e853871bd505c22_autotoc_md2291}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Using Depth Of Field}{Using Depth Of Field}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_docf2fa11f09b546737e853871bd505c22_autotoc_md2291}
{\bfseries{Depth Of Field}} uses the \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} system, so to enable and modify {\bfseries{Depth Of Field}} properties, you must add a {\bfseries{Depth Of Field}} override to a \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} in your Scene. To add {\bfseries{Depth Of Field}} to a Volume\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item In the Scene or Hierarchy view, select a Game\+Object that contains a Volume component to view it in the Inspector.
\item In the Inspector, navigate to {\bfseries{Add Override}} \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} {\bfseries{Post-\/processing}}, and click on {\bfseries{Depth Of Field}}. Universal Render Pipeline applies {\bfseries{Depth Of Field}} to any Camera this Volume affects.

The {\bfseries{Bokeh}} Depth of Field mode closely imitates the effect of a real-\/life camera. For this reason, the settings are based on real-\/life camera settings, and offer a number of properties to adjust the diaphragm blades on the Camera. For an introduction to diaphragm blades and how they affect the visual quality of your Camera output, see Improve Photography’s guide \href{https://improvephotography.com/29529/aperture-blades-many-best/}{\texttt{ Aperture Blades\+: How many is best?}}.
