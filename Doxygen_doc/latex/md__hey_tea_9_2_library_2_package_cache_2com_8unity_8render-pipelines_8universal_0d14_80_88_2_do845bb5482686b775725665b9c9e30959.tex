Chromatic Aberration creates fringes of color along boundaries that separate dark and light parts of the image. It mimics the color distortion that a real-\/world camera produces when its lens fails to join all colors to the same point. See Wikipedia\+: Chromation aberration.\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_do845bb5482686b775725665b9c9e30959_autotoc_md2282}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Using Chromatic Aberration}{Using Chromatic Aberration}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_do845bb5482686b775725665b9c9e30959_autotoc_md2282}
{\bfseries{Chromatic Aberration}} uses the \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} system, so to enable and modify {\bfseries{Chromatic Aberration}} properties, you must add a {\bfseries{Chromatic Aberration}} override to a \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} in your Scene.

To add {\bfseries{Chromatic Aberration}} to a Volume\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item In the Scene or Hierarchy view, select a Game\+Object that contains a Volume component to view it in the Inspector.
\item In the Inspector, navigate to {\bfseries{Add Override}} \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} {\bfseries{Post-\/processing}} and click on {\bfseries{Chromatic Aberration}}. Universal Render Pipeline applies {\bfseries{Chromatic Aberration}} to any Camera this Volume affects.
