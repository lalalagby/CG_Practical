 Use \href{https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.timeline@latest}{\texttt{ Timeline}} to activate, deactivate, and blend between Virtual Cameras. In \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline}, combine \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} with other Game\+Objects and assets to interactively implement and tune rich cutscenes, even interactive ones.

{\bfseries{Tip}}\+: For simple shot sequences, use a \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8cinemachine_0d2_89_87_2_documentation_0i_2_cinemachine_blend_list_camera}{Cinemachine Blend List Camera}{0} instead of \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline}.

\doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} overrides the priority-\/based decisions made by \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8cinemachine_0d2_89_87_2_documentation_0i_2_cinemachine_brain_properties}{Cinemachine Brain}{0}. When the timeline finishes, control returns to the \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Brain, which chooses the Virtual Camera with the highest Priority setting.

You control Virtual Cameras in \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} with a {\bfseries{\doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Shot Clip}}. Each shot clip points to a Virtual Camera to activate then deactivate. Use a sequence of shot clips to specify the order and duration of each shot.

To cut between two Virtual Cameras, place the clips next to each other. To blend between two Virtual Cameras, overlap the clips.



To create a \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} for \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine}\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item Create an empty Game\+Object in your Scene by choosing the \+\_\+\+\_\+\+Game\+Object \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} Create Empty \+\_\+\+\_\+menu item.
\item Give the empty Game\+Object a descriptive name. For example, {\ttfamily Intro\+Timeline}.
\item In your Scene, select your empty \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} object as the focus to create a \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} Asset and instance.
\item Click the padlock button to lock the TImeline window to make it easier to add and adjust tracks.
\item Drag a \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} camera with a Cinemachine\+Brain component onto the \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} Editor, then choose {\bfseries{Create \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Track}} from the drop-\/down menu.
\item Add other tracks to the \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} for controlling the subjects of your Scene. For example, add an Animation track to animate your main character.
\end{DoxyEnumerate}

To add \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Shot Clips to a \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Track\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item In the \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Track, right-\/click and choose {\bfseries{Add \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Shot Clip}}.
\item Do one of the following\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item To add an existing Virtual Camera to the shot clip, drag and drop it onto the Virtual Camera property in the \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Shot component.
\item To create a new Virtual Camera and add it to the shot clip, click Create in the \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Shot component.
\end{DoxyItemize}
\item In the \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} editor, adjust the order, duration, cutting, and blending of the shot clip.
\item \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8cinemachine_0d2_89_87_2_documentation_0i_2_cinemachine_virtual_camera}{Adjust the properties of the Virtual Camera}{0} to place it in the Scene and specify what to aim at or follow.
\item To animate properties of the Virtual Camera, create an Animation Track for it and animate as you would any other Game\+Object.
\item Organize your \doxylink{namespace_timeline}{Timeline} tracks to fine-\/tune your Scene. 
\end{DoxyEnumerate}
