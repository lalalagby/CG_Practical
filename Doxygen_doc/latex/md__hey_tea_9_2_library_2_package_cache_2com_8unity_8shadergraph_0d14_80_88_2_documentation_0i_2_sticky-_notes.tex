 Sticky Notes are objects in a graph view that you can write in. They are the graph view equivalent of a comment in code, and consist of a title and body. You can create as many as you want in the graph, and use them for a variety of purposes, for example\+:


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item To describe how a section of your graph works.
\item To leave notes for yourself or others collaborating in your \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} Project.
\item As a to-\/do list that includes tasks to complete at a later date.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3974}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Using Sticky Notes}{Using Sticky Notes}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3974}
To create a Sticky Note, right-\/click an empty space in the graph view and, in the context menu, click {\bfseries{Create Sticky Note}}. You can then customize and add content to the new Sticky Note. There are two text areas that you can write in\+:


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{Title}}\+: The text area at the top of the Sticky Note is the title. You can use it to concisely describe what information the Sticky Note contains.
\item {\bfseries{Body}}\+: The larger text area below the title area is the body. You can write the full contents of the note here.
\end{DoxyItemize}

\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3975}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Editing text}{Editing text}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3975}
To edit text on a Sticky Note, double-\/click on a text area. This also selects the entire text area, so be sure to move the cursor before you edit the text.\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3976}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Moving and resizing}{Moving and resizing}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3976}
You can move Sticky Notes anywhere on the graph. You can also click and drag to manually resize Sticky Notes, or have a Sticky Note automatically resize itself to fit the content. For information on how to make the Sticky Note resize itself, see {\bfseries{Fit To Text}} in the \doxylink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_context-menu}{Context menu} section below.\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3977}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Duplicating}{Duplicating}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8shadergraph_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_sticky-_notes_autotoc_md3977}
Use the following keyboard shortcuts to cut, copy, paste, and duplicate Sticky Notes.
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{Copy}}\+: Ctrl+C
\item {\bfseries{Cut}}\+: Ctrl+X
\item {\bfseries{Paste}}\+: Ctrl+V
\item {\bfseries{Duplicate}}\+: Ctrl+D
\end{DoxyItemize}
