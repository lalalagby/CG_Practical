 In your project, organize your Scene Hierarchy to have a single \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} camera with a Cinemachine\+Brain component and many Virtual Cameras.

To add a Virtual Camera to a Scene\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item In the \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} menu, choose {\bfseries{Game\+Object \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} Virtual Camera}}. ~\newline
\doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} adds a new Game\+Object with a \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Virtual Camera component. If necessary, \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} also adds a \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8cinemachine_0d2_89_87_2_documentation_0i_2_cinemachine_brain_properties}{Cinemachine Brain}{0} component to the \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} camera Game\+Object for you.
\item Use the {\bfseries{Follow}} property to specify a Game\+Object to follow. ~\newline
The Virtual Camera automatically positions the \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} camera relative to this Game\+Object at all times, even as you move it in the Scene.
\item Use the {\bfseries{Look At}} property to specify the Game\+Object that the Virtual Camera should aim at. ~\newline
The Virtual Camera automatically rotates the \doxylink{namespace_unity}{Unity} camera to face this Game\+Object at all times, even as you move it in the Scene.
\item \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8cinemachine_0d2_89_87_2_documentation_0i_2_cinemachine_virtual_camera}{Customize the Virtual Camera}{0} as needed. ~\newline
Choose the algorithm for following and looking at, and adjust settings such as the follow offset, the follow damping, the screen composition, and the damping used when re-\/aiming the camera. 
\end{DoxyEnumerate}
