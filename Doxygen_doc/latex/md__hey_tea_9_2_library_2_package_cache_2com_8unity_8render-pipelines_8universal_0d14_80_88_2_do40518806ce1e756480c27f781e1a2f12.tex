The Film Grain effect simulates the random optical texture of photographic film, usually caused by small particles being present on the physical film.\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_do40518806ce1e756480c27f781e1a2f12_autotoc_md2296}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Using Film Grain}{Using Film Grain}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_do40518806ce1e756480c27f781e1a2f12_autotoc_md2296}
{\bfseries{Film Grain}} uses the \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} framework, so to enable and modify {\bfseries{Film Grain}} properties, you must add a {\bfseries{Film Grain}} override to a \doxysectlink{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_documentation_0i_2_volumes}{Volume}{0} in your Scene. To add {\bfseries{Film Grain}} to a Volume\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item In the Scene or Hierarchy view, select a Game\+Object that contains a Volume component to view it in the Inspector.
\item In the Inspector, navigate to {\bfseries{Add Override \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} Post-\/processing}} and click on {\bfseries{Film Grain}}. URP now applies {\bfseries{Film Grain}} to any Camera this Volume affects.
\end{DoxyEnumerate}\hypertarget{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_do40518806ce1e756480c27f781e1a2f12_autotoc_md2297}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Properties}{Properties}}\label{md__hey_tea_9_2_library_2_package_cache_2com_8unity_8render-pipelines_8universal_0d14_80_88_2_do40518806ce1e756480c27f781e1a2f12_autotoc_md2297}
