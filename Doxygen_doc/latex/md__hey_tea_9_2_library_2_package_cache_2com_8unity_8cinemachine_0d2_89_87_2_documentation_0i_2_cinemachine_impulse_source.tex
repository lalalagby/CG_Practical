 Use the {\bfseries{\doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Impulse Source}} component to generate impulses on events that are not collisions or Collider triggers. This is a generic Impulse Source that exposes a family of {\ttfamily Generate\+Impulse()} API methods. These methods generate impulses at the specified locations and with the specified velocities and strengths. Call these methods directly from your game logic, or use them with \href{https://docs.unity3d.com/Manual/UnityEvents.html}{\texttt{ Unity\+Events}}.

\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>}{\bfseries{TIP\+:}} You can use the script for this component as an example to reference when creating your own custom impulse-\/generating classes.

To add a \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Impulse Source to your Scene\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item Select the Game\+Object that you want to trigger camera shake, navigate to its Inspector, and click the {\bfseries{Add Component}} button.
\item Go to {\bfseries{Scripts \texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} \doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine}}}, and select {\bfseries{\doxylink{namespace_cinemachine}{Cinemachine} Impulse Source}}.
\end{DoxyEnumerate}

{\bfseries{Impulse Type}}   &You can choose from the following Impulse types\+:~\newline
~\newline
{\bfseries{Uniform}}\+: The impulse travels with infinite speed, and will be heard at the same time and in the same way by all listeners, no matter where they are in space.~\newline
~\newline
{\bfseries{Dissipating}}\+: The strength of the impulse decreases as the distance from the source increases. Listeners that are farther away will feel a weaker signal than listeners that are closer.~\newline
~\newline
{\bfseries{Propagating}}\+: In addition to being dissipating, the impulse signal travels with finite speed outward from the source. Listeners that are farther away will feel the impulse at a later time than listeners that are close. ~\newline
